Abalindi Welfare WEDE 5020-POE

Abalindi welfare society is a non-profit organization built in 1970 in the Amatikwe community based in Durbarn,KZN. The organization primarily focuses on people over the age of 60 who have been abused or not taken care of by their family members, after a few years they decided to include children in the society of the age of 0 – 18 years old referred by district social workers for the society to take care of them.

Abalindi welfare society has ventured into different business opportunities such as creating a well known bakery in the area in order to gain funds for the organsation they further created a Creche known as the Thokozani Creche where the community could take their children to learn and they provide after care services.

This intiative created job opportunities for the community which made the organsation realise how jobs were needed in the community so they decided to open

    Block making project
    Sewing project
    Emery Farming

The intiatives at Abalindi welfare society focus on income generation and mainting ongoing projects

HIV/AIDS epidemic

With the HIV/AIDS epidemic which has signficantly effected the black community the Abalindi welfare society decided to launch a support program, in this program people who are effected directly or indirectly by HIV/AIDS are supported by recieving conseling, support group and it encourages those who are affected by it to join the programs that are offered by us This program also invites parents who have lost their children due to HIV/AIDS, helping them deal withe thier loss and provide a community around them.

Vision

“To provide preventative and institutional services for the benefit of the aged, disabled and children in need of social therapeutic and development care for all people”

Mission

“ Abalindi mission is to reach out to all communities in KZN promoting The well-being of the elderly persons by providing comfort dignified treatment, health needs accommodation to those have a need Well-being to children who are destitute, orphaned or abandoned protecting them from any abuse promoting their schooling always preparing them for a better future, providing a residential facility for those needing it.”
